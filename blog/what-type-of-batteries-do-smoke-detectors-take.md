<section><h2 id="why-certification-is-the-gate">Why certification is the gate</h2><p>For distributors, compliance is the fastest way to a delayed launch. EEC/COC and DOT are not optional paperwork. They are the market entry ticket that determines how quickly you can register and sell.</p></section>
<section><h2 id="eec-coc-at-a-glance">EEC/COC at a glance</h2><p>EEC and COC are common in EU markets. A complete COC package reduces registration friction and speeds approval across multiple countries. The key is consistency across technical specs, labels, and test reports.</p></section>
<section><h2 id="dot-for-north-america">DOT for North America</h2><p>DOT requirements focus on safety compliance and documentation alignment. Importers should confirm that labeling, lighting, and braking specs are aligned with DOT expectations before shipment.</p></section>
<section><h2 id="documents-to-request">Documents to request</h2><p>Ask for a compliance pack that includes certificates, test reports, model specs, labeling files, and VIN rules. This reduces back-and-forth with local agencies and speeds onboarding.</p></section>
<section><h2 id="how-to-reduce-approval-time">How to reduce approval time</h2><p>Work with a factory that can standardize documentation and provide localized support. The fastest approvals come from suppliers who treat compliance as part of the product, not a last-minute task.</p></section>
