<h1>What Type of Batteries Do Smoke Detectors Take?</h1>

<p>Smoke detectors primarily use 9V and AA batteries, with some models incorporating AAA or sealed 10-year lithium batteries. 9V batteries have traditionally been favored for their compact size and reliable power but often require more frequent replacement, typically every six months to a year. AA batteries, commonly used in newer models, offer longer life and easier availability. Lithium batteries, especially sealed 10-year types, provide a maintenance-free option, but come at a higher upfront cost. Choosing the right battery involves balancing longevity, cost, and maintenance convenience. Regular testing and timely replacement ensure optimal smoke detector functionality and home safety.</p>

<article class="order-1 lg:order-none">
      <section><h2 id="common-battery-types-for-smoke-detectors">Common Battery Types for Smoke Detectors</h2><p>Most smoke detectors use either 9V or AA batteries. Historically, 9V batteries were the standard owing to their compact shape and compatibility with older designs. Recently, many models have adopted two or three AA batteries to extend battery life and improve availability. Some smoke detectors also use AAA batteries or specialized lithium types, depending on the design and power requirements. The battery type influences the device's maintenance frequency and reliability.</p><h3>9V Batteries</h3><p>9V batteries have been the go-to choice for smoke alarms for many years. They are rectangular with snap connectors, making them easy to install in dedicated smoke detectors. These batteries typically provide good power but tend to have shorter life spans compared to AA alternatives.</p><h3>AA Batteries</h3><p>More recent smoke detector models often use two or three AA batteries. AA batteries generally offer longer life, are widely available, and easier to replace. Lithium AA batteries, in particular, have superior longevity, making them ideal for smoke detectors.</p>
